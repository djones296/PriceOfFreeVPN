When looking at Data Privacy there are two main laws relating to the Data Privacy of those within the UK, these are the Data Protection Act of 1998 and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) of 2018.

The Data Protection Act is made up of several key principles which any organisation holding personal data of individuals, be they customers, staff, business partners, etc must abide by to ensure that the individual’s data is kept safe and secure.

These principles ensure that data is:

1.       Only used for its originally intended purpose

2.       Kept Safe and Secure

3.       Used only in ways that adhere to the law

4.       Not transferred outside of the European Economic Area

5.       Not stored longer than it is necessary to do so

6.       Stored following the rules and regulations regarding people’s data protection rights

7.       The individual whose data is being stored has the right to be forgotten (the company must delete all data held on the individual if asked to do so)

 

The GDPR is a set of legal guidelines for the collection and processing of personal information of individuals within the European Union (EU). The GDPR sets out the key principles of data management and the rights of the individual regarding what data is held on them. The GDPR although set within the EU holds the right to impose its laws on any company operating within the EU holding private information on any EU citizen. The GDPR has the right to inflict heavy fines on companies failing to adhere to its policies. Although many of the issues targeted by the GDPR are also targeted in the Data Protection Act there are some added extras which help to further secure the individuals Data Privacy.

Some examples of these include:

1.       The guidelines include both social networks and cloud storage systems

2.       If data is to be transferred outside of the EU the country the data is being sent to must guarantee the same level of data protection as those within the EU do

3.       The user must be made aware of the request to store private information and its intended purpose in a clear and easily understood way
